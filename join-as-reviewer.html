Join as Reviewer

AJRST's Editorial Board consists of an Editor-in-Chief and reviewers who are the Editorial Board members appointed from a global pool of specialists renowned for their exceptional academic qualifications and expertise within the journal's specific field. The composition of the Editorial Board is flexible, allowing for a varied number of members. Those selected for the board must fulfill the following criteria:

  • Bachelors/Masters/ Ph.D. degree in relevant subjects covered in AJRST
  • Excellent academic records (Honours Degree holders is a bonus)
  • Proficient verbal and written communication skills in English
  • Entry/Experienced, proficient in MS Word. Experience with research methodologies/published academic papers is an added bonus

Editorial Board responsibilities:

  1. Adherence to Editorial Direction and Ethics: Editorial Board members must adhere to the direction set by the Editor-in-Chief, ensuring transparency, fairness, and confidentiality throughout the peer review and publication process. They should treat authors and reviewers with dignity and respect, maintaining impartiality and honesty in their judgments.

  2. Assignment Responsiveness and Conflict Management: Upon assignment of articles in their area of expertise, Editorial Board members should promptly accept or decline based on personal conflicts of interest, notifying the Editorial Office accordingly. They are responsible for managing potential conflicts transparently and responsibly.

  3. Efficient Peer Review Management: Editors oversee a fast and transparent peer review process, selecting appropriate reviewers, and ensuring timely feedback to authors. They maintain confidentiality of author and reviewer information throughout the review process. Reviewers need to thoroughly check the manuscripts they receive from the Chief Editor and provide a completed marks sheet (which is out of 100) for each manuscript, assessing its relevance, degree of plagiarism, and content.

  4. Publication Decision and Integrity: Editors play a crucial role in making final decisions on manuscript publication, providing clear justifications and ensuring adherence to journal policies. They handle queries regarding editorial decisions promptly and maintain the integrity of the publication process.

  5. Contribution to Journal Development: Editorial Board members actively contribute to journal development by suggesting timely topics, potential guest editors, and attending editorial meetings as required. They collaborate closely with the Editorial Office and publishing house to ensure smooth operations and compliance with publishing guidelines.
